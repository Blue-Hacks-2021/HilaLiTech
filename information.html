Ano ba ang bagyo?

Tropical cyclones are cyclonic circulations that occur when the water evaporated from any part of the sea (except for the sea which surface temperature is cool) is carried up into the atmosphere and condenses, forming clouds from which all forms of precipitation results. These are warm-core low-pressure systems associated with a spiral inflow of mass at the bottom level and a spiral outflow at the top level. The energy of the tropical cyclone, therefore, is derived from the massive liberation of the latent heat of condensation. Tropical Cyclones usually developed at latitudes greater than 5 from the equator. They reach their greatest intensity when located in warm water and become weaker when moving inland. The Philippines is prone to these tropical cyclones because of its geographical location.
